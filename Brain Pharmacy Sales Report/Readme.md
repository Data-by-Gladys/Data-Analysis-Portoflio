### BRAIN PHARMACY REPORT
# Introduction:
This report analyzes sales data for Brain Pharmacy for the year 2017 and 2018. The analysis evaluates product performance, geographic distribution, yearly trends, and representative efficiency to identify growth drivers and areas for improvement.

# Objectives:
The objective of this analysis report is to uncover actionable insights from the Brain Pharmacy sales record, specifically focusing on high performance products and yearly sales trends.

# Data Cleaning & Methodology:
Tools: Microsoft Excel (Power Query, Pivot Tables, and Pivot Charts)
Process: Excel Power query was employed for the data cleaning and transformation, duplicate entries were removed, Missing values were handled by either filling or removal, All inconsistent text format and data types were corrected to improve accuracy.  The pivot table was created to analyze trends, observe data records. Visualization was developed using excel charts to further display findings in a clear and understandable format. 

# Key insights & findings:
Notable insights extracted from the dataset include:
Total Revenue: The total sales recorded across both years is 535,272.
Yearly Sales Trend: There was massive growth in 2018. Sales rose from 28,817 in 2017 to 506,455 in 2018.
Top Product Performance: Sumanazole is the highest-selling product, significantly outperforming others in the catalog.
Geographic Performance: Poland is the dominant market, contributing 468,123 in sales, while Germany contributed 67,149.
Low Performing Products: Several products, including Effidomide Evofribrate and Clindaracil, show near-zero sales volume, indicating a lack of demand or supply chain issues.
Channel Efficiency: The Retail and Private channels are the primary drivers of volume, specifically led by sales reps Delta and Charlie.
# Conclusion
The analysis reveals that Brain Pharmacy experienced growth in 2018, with sales increasing compared to 2017. This increase is largely driven by the high demand for the product, Sumanazole.
However, the data also highlights a significant list of underperforming products that aren’t generating negligible revenue. To sustain this sales growth, the Brain Pharmacy should focus on Poland and the high demanding product sector while investigating whether the low-performing products should be discontinued or rebranded to optimize inventory costs.
